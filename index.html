About Me

Faith Njahira Wangari is a disability and inclusive education scholar expert with muscular dystrophy. She has a Bachelor’s degree in Education (Special needs education) from Kenyatta University, a certificate Functional and Educational Assessment from Kenya Institute of Special Education (KISE) and a Master of Science in Teaching and Curriculum (Inclusive Education and Disability Rights) from Syracuse University. She has taught as both a teacher-in-training as well as a graduate teacher in primary and secondary schools for more than two years.

More About Me

Njahira has been a guest lecturer in undergraduate and graduate classrooms on disability. Additionally, she has worked as the disability focal person in both local and international organisations with various levels of advising around education for learners.

Focus

Guiding/Supporting you as you make informed education and life decisions for your learner with and without disability.

Why

The current education system in the country can be challenging for families (by birth, adoption or by love) to navigate. Often times, the adults making decisions with regard to education are influenced and guided by their own past experiences in whatever education system they may have gone through as well as perceptions of what other systems may have been providing.

What

Coaching/advising allows you a space to examine the education options of the learner concerned with all the relevant information. It would involve provision of information about what should influence the education decisions of learners regardless of their age, genders, physical location while also taking into account these very variables.

Elders accessibility and support

As people age, their bodies are less agile and require more support in their daily life to avoid and limit the risk of injury. Often times, newly retired people are among those who might find themselves spending more time in their own homes than ever before. The risks of falls and need for additional movement support increase. This service aims to offer guidance and support to them and their loved ones as they navigate their environments in ageing bodies and maintain their agency and independence safely.

Depending on individual need, the services offered may include;

  • • Bathroom safety and comfort
  • • Removing fall and injury risks at home
  • • Accessibility and home modifications
  • • Emergency planning and support
  • • Footwear and back support (Gait support)
